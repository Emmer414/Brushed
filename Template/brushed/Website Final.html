FRC Sub-Teams

Administrative

Team Captain: Ashley Brock

The administrative team is responsible for running team meetings, organizing public relations and coordinating Fusion’s involvement in the community. Their efforts include sharing the team’s mission with officials, professional organizations and potential members and working on building managerial skills to use in the future.

Awards
The awards sub-team is in charge of ensuring that all awards are applied for and each sub-team is meeting their milestones on time. These individuals keep our team on track and oversee every sub-team in managerial positons.

Cost Accounting
The cost accounting sub-team tracks our costs and mantains the bill of materials. This mathematics-based team concentrates on tracking all expenses and building skills that will be useful in the future.

Team Member(s):
Austin Michaels (Triangle)
Financial
The financial sub-team oversees all business-related work as well as formulating our team business plan and keeping the team under budget.

Communications

Team Captain: Tatianna Myers

The communications sub-team is responsible for portraying Team Fusion to the world. They are in charge of any and all technology-based activities by keeping up the website, designing 3D parts and flyers, and creating our team videos.

Graphic Design
The graphic design team is the sub-team responsible for all of Team Fusion’s logos, brochures and other aesthetic needs. This team is important as their designs are used for the website, social media and all of Team Fusion’s specialty items.

Video
Video team is tasked with capturing every moment and memory of what Team Fusion does. Every event, build day, and community service act is captured in pictures and videos. These videos and pictures are used by the website team, graphics team, and public relations team to present all of Team Fusion’s progress and deeds. This sub-team is also in charge of creating the videos to present for the Chairman’s Award.

Technical

Team Captain: Austin Michaels "Triangle"

The technical sub-team is responsible for the development of the robot and practice field. These tactile students program, wire, assemble, and build the prototypes, field and robot each year while learning how to use the power tools and act in a shop environment

Systems
Systems includes mechanical, electrical, drive and pneumatic sub-teams. They are directly responsible for designing, building, wiring and controlling the robot every year.
