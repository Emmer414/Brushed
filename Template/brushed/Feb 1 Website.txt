					<h3 class="profile-name" style= text-align:center>About Mississippi's rising robotics team #364</h3>
            

						<p class="profile-description" >Fourteen years have brought about many challenges and breakthroughs for Mississippi’s premiere FIRST Robotics team. Team Fusion #364 remains active in the community to enrich all people- young and old, big and small -with technology, science and cutting-edge information. Knowing that one day the world will be dependent on today’s young people, the students and mentors of Team Fusion strive to change the world in which we live and work.

Through community outreach, fundraisers, mentor programs and other events and demonstrations, Team Fusion reaches out to our community in an attempt to positively affect and mold the world. Since its conception, Team Fusion has concentrated on community involvement in an effort to help all people around us and to alter the world to make it a better, more successful place.

Team Fusion and FIRST encompass a need for business-oriented skills, knowledge, commitment and determination.

Operating Team Fusion, like all other FIRST teams, is rather expensive when all materials, travel expenses and extra events are factored into the budget. To meet these steep monetary needs, we require tremendous effort and commitment from the parent booster club, students, engineers and local businesses.

Team Fusion realizes that in order to be successful, we must have sound business principles and teamwork in all endeavors.

Team Fusion strives to be the best it can be in all challenges and undertakings. Our mission is to spread the idea of FIRST and to inspire other teams and individuals to excel in all aspects of science, technology and business.

This initiative inspired us to assist and mentor FIRST teams in Ecuador and Israel over the 2004 and 2005 competitions.

In 2008, the team assisted the “Kiwis,” a team from New Zealand starting a program. Team Fusion also helped create a team in Brazil, requiring two members to learn Portuguese. Joe Daschbach, expressing his appreciation for Team Fusion’s assistance said, “We have three rookie teams in New Orleans this year, and Team Fusion played a big part in making that happen!”

Last year, Team Fusion helped start an FLL Team at Anniston Elementary (a local elementary school) as well as an FTC Team at Gulfport High School. In addition, we helped rookie team #4107 at West Harrison High School get on their feet by sharing our facilities, as well as sending our members to their school to help them whenever they need us.

As a team, we work to overcome all obstacles, problems, adversity and hardships while still having fun! We are rewarded not only with prestigious awards, but with a sense of satisfaction for doing something great that can really make a difference. Few things provide these opportunities as well as FIRST Robotics. Team Fusion embodies all ideals of FIRST and tries to spread the FIRST fever to others. <a href="#"></a>.
</p>

<h3 class="profile-name" style=text-align:center>Learn about the eco-friendly 2015 FRC game</h3>
        

<p class="profile-description">FIRST Stronghold is the 2016 FIRST Robotics Competition game for 2016. In this game, robots are used to cross “castle walls” – different obstacles outlining the “courtyard” – and score “boulders” into the castle towers at the opposite end of the field. This is played on a 27 ft. x 54 ft. field. Points are scored by crossing elements of the tower’s outer works, shooting boulders into the opposing tower’s five goals in order to lower the tower strength, and by surrounding and scaling the tower.

This years game has a hidden meaning, it heavily promotes communication and working with constraints and obstacles. The drivers must communicate with their “spy” or exclusively drive through on-board cameras. This will challenge many people to work together between their own team members and with others.

To find out more about this year’s game, watch the video below or go the official FIRST website.

May your castles be strong!</a>.

<h3 class="profile-name" style=text-align:center>FIRST: For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology</h3>
            

<p class="profile-description"> <a href="#"></a>.

Dean Kamen’s vision of FIRST is “to transform our culture by creating a world where science and technology are celebrated, and where young people dream of becoming science and technology heroes.”
FIRST is an internationally acclaimed organization that hosts an annual competition which allows more than 200,000 high school students to compete against other teams on a global scale.

However, unlike other competitions in which winning by any means is the only option, FIRST holds a standard of “gracious professionalism,” where teams share ideas, strategies and even help repair other teams’ robots.

This emulates the respect and business ethics expected from corporations in the real world. In contrast to most athletic sports where the sport itself is the main focus, FIRST centers around influencing and affecting the community in the most positive ways it can (whether the “community” is a neighborhood or the world). “Coopertition” among students creates a willingness to embark on the hard, long journey of building and battling amongst groups of peers, all brought together by FIRST Robotics. “FIRST is the vehicle – students are the rocket fuel.”

For more information regarding FIRST’s history, please visit the official FIRST website.</p>
